About Us

Aim:-

International Conference on Advances in Mechanical Processing and Design – 2019 (ICAMPD-2019) is drafted as a global forum for sharing, discussing and improving the latest innovative research on facilitating aspects of Mechanical Engineering. ICAMPD-2019 widens the scope of bringing together innovators, researchers and industries under common goal – creating, evaluating, implementing and benefiting from innovations. It will thus support innovative projects and bring benefits to all involved participants. Participants from universities, institutes, associations, companies, consultancies, etc. are invited. ICAMPD-2019 aims to be one of the most influential channel transferring innovative ideas from academia to industry thereby these ideas may start to generate consultancy, projects and collaborations.

ICAMPD-2019Tech-Expo is for participants from academia, R&D organizations and industries to exhibit their potential through posters and model/prototype designed by them (if any), highlighting their innovation in the field of mechanical engineering. Innovations which may have potential for filing patents will be identified and will be mentored further for IPR filing.

About KIIT & KISS

Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology (KIIT) is a deemed to be university located at Bhubaneswar, Odisha, India. KIIT is unique among its peer institutions in having nineteen schools co-located on one contiguous campus offering more than 100programs including UG/PG studies in the discipline including engineering, management, medicine and law. KIIT has been awarded Tier-1 status by NBA (AICTE), accredited with grade ‘A’ by NAAC (UGC), Ranked by NIRF, Govt. of India and has been accredited by IET, U.K. It has sister institution, Kalinga Institute of Social Science (KISS) has the distinction of being the largest and first tribal university of the world. KIIT & KISS have been founded by eminent educationist and social activist Prof. Achyuta Samanta. Recently KISS has been selected to host the 19th International Congress of Anthropological and Ethnological Sciences (ICAES) in 2023.

Bhubaneswar “Temple City of India”

With the architectural legacy of the Kalinga dynasty, Bhubaneswar boasts up with hundreds of temples including Lingaraj, Mukteswar, Raja Rani, Brahmeswar, Vital etc. dating from 6th-13th century AD. Apart from remarkably scculpured temples, the Jain caves of Khandagiri and Udayagiri, Nadankanan Zoological park, the peace pagoda ‘Dhauli’ on the bank of river Daya attract visitors from all over the world. Puri, Konark along with capital city of Bhubaneswar form the three vertices of Golden Triangle of Odisha tourism circuit.
